# OmicsTrust Claim Boundary

## Supported

- A structural transcriptomic signal was detected under the recorded analysis.
- The signal passed or failed the reported null, stability, and confounding checks.
- A treatment-response interaction is an internally supported retrospective
  research hypothesis when its statistics and corrections pass their gates.
- The result can be packaged as a locked contract for independent validation.

## Not Supported Without Independent Validation

- Clinical biomarker, diagnostic, prognostic, or treatment-selection claims.
- Therapeutic efficacy, safety, mechanism, or patient-benefit claims.
- A compact gene panel derived post hoc.
- Generalization outside the analyzed cohort.
- FDA, regulatory, or clinical-readiness claims.

## PC11-Specific Boundary

The preserved VANISH result supports a strong internal PC11/vasopressor
interaction hypothesis. Re-running the same VANISH cohort demonstrates
reproducibility, not external validation. The primary next step is a locked test
in an independent cohort with transcriptomics, randomized vasopressor labels,
28-day outcome, and prespecified covariates.
